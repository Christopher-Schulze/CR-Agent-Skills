# Name Research Checklist

Use this checklist when moving from generated candidates to a shortlist that can survive real-world scrutiny.

## Domain Checks

Use at least two live sources for finalists:

- RDAP, WHOIS, ICANN Lookup, or a registry-specific lookup.
- A registrar search page such as Namecheap, GoDaddy, Cloudflare Registrar, Porkbun, or the relevant registry.
- Direct DNS/HTTP or browser visit to the exact domain.
- Web search for the exact domain in quotes.

Classify domain status:

- `likely available`: no registration signal from RDAP/WHOIS and registrar reports normal availability.
- `registered`: RDAP/WHOIS record exists, registrar says unavailable, or an active/parked site resolves.
- `premium/aftermarket`: purchasable only through a premium or resale listing.
- `unclear`: conflicting signals, timeout, rate limit, unsupported TLD, or suspicious parking state.

## Competitor Checks

Search each finalist across:

- Google/Bing exact quote search.
- Exact quoted name plus the product category.
- Domain slug, with and without TLD.
- Similar spellings, plurals, dropped vowels, fuzzy variants, and homophones.
- Product Hunt, GitHub, LinkedIn, Crunchbase, app stores, social handles, local business directories, and category directories where relevant.

Prioritize collision risk by market proximity, not raw result count.

Classify collision risk:

- `direct`: same or near-identical name in the same category.
- `adjacent`: same or near-identical name in a related category.
- `distant`: same name in an unrelated market.
- `noise`: weak, generic, inactive, or irrelevant match.

## Trademark Checks

For US-facing projects, search USPTO. For EU-facing projects, search EUIPO. For international brand rollouts, also search WIPO Global Brand Database. Summarize obvious conflicts, but never present the result as legal clearance.

## Final Recommendation Standard

A strong name should be:

- Easy to say after hearing once.
- Easy to spell after hearing once.
- Distinctive in the category.
- Flexible if the product expands.
- Available or realistically acquirable under the user's domain strategy.
- Low-risk against active same-market competitors.
